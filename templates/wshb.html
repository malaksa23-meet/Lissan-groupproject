Women Speaking Hebrew at Ben Gurion University

Women Speaking Hebrew at Ben Gurion University

“Speaking Hebrew at Ben-Gurion University” is a joint pilot project of the Lissan Association, the Ajik Association of the Negev Institute, and the Department of Social Involvement at the Ben-Gurion University of the Negev. The aim of the project is to provide useful and practical Hebrew to Bedouin women in the Negev, in order to remove the language barrier that makes it difficult for them to conduct themselves independently, exercise their rights, gain access to the resources they deserve and integrate into the employment market. For this project, we adapted our study materials to the Negev region as well as to Bedouin culture and society.

In December 2021 we opened the first course of the pilot program in the Arab-Bedouin locality of al-Zarnug. Throughout the activity year, the studies take place in al-Zarnog with a visit or two to the Ben-Gurion campus for practice, enrichment, and experience.

Following the pilot, we aim to open more groups in the communities near Be’er Sheva and on the Ben-Gurion campus.
