Our Team

Our Staff

Talia Vekshtein

CEO


As a project manager and social activist, Talia has years of experience in informal education and Israeli-Palestinian social initiatives. Her relationship with Lissan started three years ago as a volunteer teacher in Lissan's Medabrot Ivrit (Women Speaking Hebrew) program, and continued with her role as COO, and now CEO. Talia holds a B.A. in General and Comparative Literature and was part of the "Amirim Honors Program in the Humanities" at the Hebrew University. She is currently working towards an MFA in Documentary Cinema at Tel Aviv University.

Email Talia

Rivka Cohen

Director of Partnerships and Strategic Development

In addition to her role at Lissan, Rivka is also the Managing Editor of the anthology Monologues from the Makom: Intertwined Narratives of Sexuality, Gender, Body Image, and Jewish Identity. Prior to her role at Lissan, Rivka served as the Program Manager at JOFA, the Jewish Orthodox Feminist Alliance, in New York. Rivka holds a B.A. in Islamic & Middle Eastern Studies and Near Eastern & Judaic Studies from Brandeis University.
Email Rivka

Natasha Voloshin 

COO
Natasha is studying towards her B.A. in Political Science, Sociology, and Anthropology at the Hebrew University, with a specialization at the NGO Greenpeace Israel. Previously, she served as a Research Assistant at the Jerusalem Institute for Policy Studies, and as a delegate of the Hebrew University Student Union. Natasha also volunteered for Lissan as a Hebrew teacher in our Medabrot Ivrit program.
Email Natasha

Mina Hashemeha

Field Director of “A Taste of Hebrew” Program

Mina holds a BA in Communication from Al Quds University and an MBA from the Ono Academic College. Mina has previously served as a Program Manager for various projects in East Jerusalem, including the "Healthy Living" program at the Tzur Bahar Community Center and the "Updating Addresses" program through the Jerusalem Municipality.
Email Mina

Rahaf Al-Saad

Medabrot Ivrit Coordinator at Har Hatzofim

Rahaf Al-Saad, a native Jerusalemite, is the Medabrot Ivrit - Women Speaking Hebrew program’s on-campus coordinator. This is her third year in the role. Rahaf is studying towards an M.A. in Education- honors program - at the Hebrew University, and works as a teaching assistant at the Education school at the Hebrew University. Rahaf wrote an Arabic novel three years ago and is currently working on her second book. She holds a B.A. in Education and Arabic Language and Literature from the Hebrew University.
Email Rahaf

Ronit Ben Ari

Director of Pedagogy
Ronit Ben Ari holds a Masters in Language Education in Multicultural Communities from Levinsky College. Ronit has years of experience developing Hebrew as a second language programming for Arabic speakers and for schools in the diaspora. She has also advised and taught teachers in Israel and around the world on Hebrew pedagogy. Among other projects, Ronit has experience leading groups of coordinators and Hebrew teachers from East Jerusalem.
Email Ronit

Yuli Hatzofe

Director of Pedagogy

Yuli Holds a B.A. in Education and Political Science from the Hebrew University and a Certificate of Teaching Hebrew as a Second Language from the Rothberg International School. Prior to her role at Lissan, Yuli managed a Hebrew Ulpan for asylum seekers at the African Community Center in Jerusalem and worked in the Labor and Social Welfare Department at the Headquarters for Promoting Arab Employment.

Yael Gaulan

Pedagogical Staff
Yael Gaulan is a certified Hebrew as a second language teacher and is currently pursuing her doctorate in Linguistics at the Hebrew University. She is also a professor for Hebrew Education at Middlebury College in Vermont. Yael is involved in pedagogical research and the development of Hebrew teaching materials for Arabic-speakers, asylum seekers, and others. She is a staff member of the Advanced Teacher Training Program for Hebrew and Arabic Teachers, run by Lissan and Madrasa.

Liora Tamir

Pedagogical Staff
Liora Tamir is a doctorate student for International Education at New York University, specializing in language education in regions of conflict. Liora is responsible for the development of teaching materials at Lissan, including curricula for Lissan’s “Know Your Rights” and “Career Advancement” courses. Liora has taught Hebrew as a second language at the University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia, in Israel, and in schools throughout East Jerusalem.

Shlomit Fox

Pedagogical Coordinator, Medabrot Ivrit Level ‘א

Shlomit is a Master's student studying Hebrew Language at the Hebrew University. In her first degree, she studied in the Hebrew Language department and in the Multidisciplinary Program in the Humanities. Shlomit lives in Jerusalem and has been teaching in Hebrew ulpans throughout the city for the past five years.

Avital Ende

Pedagogical Coordinator, Medabrot Ivrit Level ב'1

Avital is a native Jerusalemite and a graduate student in the Department of Art History. She is certified in teaching Hebrew as a second language from the “Ivrit+” program, a program aiming to integrate Arab populations into the workforce, sponsored by the Ministry of Labor, Welfare and Social Services, in conjunction with JDC-Tevet. Avital teaches Hebrew in various settings and is a pedagogical coordinator in Medabrot Ivrit. Last year, Avital was a Hebrew teacher in Medabrot Ivrit.

Hala Makhoul

Pedagogical Coordinator, Medabrot Ivrit Level ב'2

She previously managed Shekel's curricula and today is developing a learning center for people with special needs in East Jerusalem. Previously, Hala taught Hebrew in East Jerusalem as part of Axioma.

Board of Directors

Alumah Dor

Alumah, the chairperson of the Lissan board, is a fellow at the Institute of Halakhic Leadership at Midreshet Lindenbaum. She holds an M.A. in History from the Hebrew University.

Dr. Samira Alayan

Dr. Samira Alayan is a senior lecturer at the Seymour Fox School of Education at the Hebrew University and a tenured lecturer and teacher trainer at the David Yellin Teacher College in Jerusalem. Samira specializes in education in conflict societies, and textbooks and identity in the Middle East.

Yoni Levi

Yoni holds a B.A. in Islamic and Middle Eastern Studies from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem and an M.A. in Human Rights Studies from Columbia University in New York. He is the Acting Director, on behalf of Axioma for the Ministry of Education, of the “Educational Spaces” program, promoting and forming schools' uniqueness and identity in Israel.

Roni Matus-Miller

Roni holds a B.A. in Islamic and Middle Eastern Studies from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, a Teacher Certification in History Studies from the Teach First Israel Program (HOTAM), and a M.Ed. in Multidisciplinary Studies in the Humanities from Oranim Academic College.
