Accessing Rights and Resources

Independent Study Guides:

The increasing demand for learning Hebrew in the face of the scarcity of accessible frameworks, economically, culturally, or pragmatically, has led us to think of other solutions besides the Hebrew classes themselves. Many women in the east of the city have to deal with the Israeli bureaucracy, which in many cases is accessible only in Hebrew. This situation led us to open a unique course on the exercising of rights and the practice of Hebrew for “Speaking Hebrew” students together with the women’s community in Jerusalem, the Department of Exercising Rights, and the Digital Communities Initiative in the Jerusalem Municipality. The course includes a series of sessions on topics such as getting to know the All Rights website and finding information about rights, dealing with Social Security, labor law for women, medical rights, financial management and more. In the summer of 2021, we conducted a pilot of the course for Lower Intermediate graduates, and throughout the 2021-2022 activity year, there will be two cycles of the course that will be open to all students of the “Speak Hebrew” project. The students who will be accepted to the course, in parallel with their Hebrew studies, will become ambassadors of rights and can volunteer with individual assistance and transfer the knowledge and tools they acquired in the course to their communities, both within the framework of rights enforcement centers in the east of the city and within the framework of the community of Lissan.

During the Coronavirus Pandemic

During the Coronavirus pandemic, the Jerusalem community faced many challenges and increased bureaucratic demands around issues such as medicine and employment. Unfortunately, there was a severe lack of accessible information in Arabic. In response, Lissan sought to make such vital information in Hebrew more accessible to Arabic-speakers. At the outbreak of the pandemic, we started compiling vocabulary worksheets and recordings on useful and urgent topics such as medical care. Additionally, in partnership with the The Clinical Legal Education Center at the Hebrew University, we have created a document that centralizes useful links regarding the Coronavirus situation for residents of East Jerusalem. Now more than ever, we are continuing our collaborative work to provide additional relevant content for our students and wider community, and to make necessary bureaucratic procedures more accessible to East Jerusalemites.

Collaboration with Workers Advice Center - Ma'an

As part of our mission to increase access to rights and resources alongside Hebrew language proficiency, Lissan collaborates with the Workers Advice Center – Ma’an to provide guided workshops in Arabic and Hebrew on employment rights and bureaucratic procedures. Together we also developed mini-guides in both Hebrew and Arabic ​​with useful vocabulary, phrases, and practice sentences relevant to employment in Hebrew, as well as useful links for the benefit of the wider community

Special thanks to Liora Tamir for her content development work on these topics
