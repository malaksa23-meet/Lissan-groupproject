Lissan - ליסאן - لسان

Lissan (Arabic for language or tongue) is a nonprofit organization that promotes gender and social equality in Jerusalem through language education. Lissan’s flagship program, Medabrot Ivrit, offers practical Hebrew classes for East Jerusalemite women to provide them with greater access to rights, resources, and opportunities. Through practical and accessible Hebrew classes for women, pedagogical and cultural sensitivity training for teachers, and joint programming for Israeli and Palestinian volunteers, Lissan aims to bridge the gap between East and West Jerusalem and strengthen East Jerusalemite women. Lissan’s long term vision is for East Jerusalemite women to have ultimate agency in their personal and professional lives, and for the city of Jerusalem – both East and West – to become a more just and equal space for all of its residents.

"What is "Lissan?

Lissan is the Arabic word for “language” or “tongue” (similar to the word “lashon” in Hebrew). We chose this name to represent our organization because it reflects our vision of Jerusalem as a bilingual space, and our mission to promote linguistic justice and maximum accessibility for all residents of the city.

What is “linguistic justice?”

The concept of “linguistic justice” developed around the idea of making translations accessible for basic resources such as medical and legal services. The term “justice” in this context expresses that the lack of accessibility is neither accidental nor transparent, and that full accessibility is the basis for an egalitarian and just society. The right to understand and to be understood, to communicate in a language in which you are comfortable speaking, to receive the full extent of information and access to your rights regardless of your language – in tandem with systemic recognition of these rights – are all part of our worldview. Language is a central element of our identities, as well as a key tool for communication, and there is no justice without recognition of the importance of language as an integral part of who we are. Unfortunately, we still have a long road ahead towards creating a diverse society in which accessibility of language is readily available for all identities. In a city where communities speak different languages, linguistic justice means actively pursuing strategies that will advance the above principles in practice. Lissan strategically promotes these ideals by providing essential Hebrew language skills to women, Arabic classes to medical personnel, and access to vital information in both languages.

Come volunteer with us!

Medabrot Ivrit – Women Speaking Hebrew is looking for volunteer teachers for the new school year! Lissan’s Medabrot Ivrit program provides women from East Jerusalem with practical Hebrew language skills. Working in pairs, each team of volunteers leads a weekly course for women, in the evening or morning, at a set location in Jerusalem, over the course of the academic year. If you have a desire to make Jerusalem a better place to live in, and you have experience in teaching or informal education, we invite you to join us.

Lissan's beginner-level workbooks are now available for purchase!​

Lissan’s beginner-level workbook was developed by professional educators with experience teaching Hebrew at the Hebrew University in Jerusalem and in Ulpan programs throughout Israel. These educators also have vast experience working with and teaching Arabic-speaking populations. The workbook is intended for anyone whose Hebrew is at the beginner level, whether they are in high school, university, or adults. The workbook includes references to Arab culture and society, and takes into account cultural sensitivities of the population.
